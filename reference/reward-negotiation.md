# Reward & Payout Negotiation

How negotiating a better deal out of a client gets resolved. Established 2026-07-22.

## Baseline: freeform
Once a job's outcome and a proposed payout are on the table, swapping the *composition* of the reward (cash for gear, splitting differently, etc.) stays freeform — no roll, just asking for what's wanted, as with Bull's Point-Defense Battery and Doc's Precision Rifle.

## Actually negotiating a better deal: one roll, no default fail
When squeezing more out of a client — not just picking what form the reward takes — it costs one trigger roll (Pull Rank, Threaten, Show Off, Charm, whatever fits the approach). Unlike most checks, there's **no default failure state**: the roll (1–20, plus any applicable bonus) reads as a sliding scale from a bad outcome to an amazing one. A low roll doesn't blow the deal, it just means the client doesn't budge much or the terms come with a minor catch.

## When it does become risky
If the ask gets **pushed** — overplaying the hand, demanding something outrageous, threatening someone who can actually retaliate, a bluff that could be caught — the GM can call for a real DC with genuine failure consequences instead of the sliding scale. Get a read on whether the player is pushing it before defaulting to the safe version.
