# Interrogation Resolution

How interrogating a captured NPC gets resolved. Established 2026-07-22.

## Two rolls

**Roll 1 — Gate (success/fail).** A trigger check (whatever fits the approach — Threaten, Take Someone Out, Read a Situation, etc.) or a flat 1d20 if nothing fits, against a GM-set DC reflecting the target's resistance, loyalty, or fear.
- **Fail:** the target stonewalls. No information (unless the GM has a specific reason to hand over a scrap regardless — that's discretion, not a rule).
- **Success:** proceed to Roll 2.

**Roll 2 — Benefit (only on a Roll 1 success).** A plain 1d20 (plus any bonus that logically applies), read as a sliding scale of how much/how good the information is:
- **Low roll:** vague, minimal, or already-half-known information.
- **Mid roll:** solid, actionable intel.
- **High roll (nat 20 territory):** comprehensive information, possibly including something beyond what was actually asked.

No fixed numeric breakpoints — read the roll's position on the 1–20 range and scale the payoff narratively, the same way it's been handled in play so far.
