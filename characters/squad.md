# The Squad

Lee's team — a small independent mercenary outfit operating on the frontier. Full mech stat blocks will be filled in before each character's first real fight; for now, roles and personalities are locked.

## Voss "Doc" Amara — Support
**Mech:** IPS-N Lancaster (Support — agile, carries allies into the fight and repairs them mid-combat)

Late 40s, unhurried, missing two fingers on her left hand and never explains how. Was a trauma surgeon on a core world before a manna shortfall gutted her hospital's funding and she went where the money still moved: the frontier. Doesn't flinch, doesn't preach, but keeps a running tally of who owes her a favor for patching them up. The closest thing the crew has to a conscience — she's seen what happens when contracts go bad and mercs stop asking who's paying them.

Dry, economical with words. Calls Lee "sniper boy" without a hint of affection or malice — it's just what he is to her until proven otherwise.

## Kessa "Bull" Draye — Defender
**Mech:** IPS-N Drake (Defender — walking fortress, overarm shield, rotary cannon)

Broad, scarred, ex-Union navy who walked away after a peacekeeping op turned into something she couldn't square with the word "peace." Doesn't talk about it. Holds the line, plants herself between the team and whatever's shooting at them, and considers that the whole of her job description. Suspicious of corporate contracts, more suspicious of anyone who trusts them completely — including, pointedly, Lee, whose read on any job is "can I get paid and get out clean."

Gruff, protective, slow to warm up. Respects competence over charm, which means Lee has to work for it.

## Jonas "Riot" Achebe — Striker
**Mech:** IPS-N Nelson (Striker — fast, aggressive hit-and-run melee)

Early 20s, the newest to the crew, desperate to prove the outfit was right to take him on. Grew up on a Diasporan colony that got quietly deprioritized by Union relief after a bad harvest cycle — joined up the first chance he got, chasing purpose as much as pay. Reckless, funny, wears his heart where everyone can see it, which needles Lee's carefully-cultivated cool. Follows Lee's marks and hacks into the kill without hesitation — the two of them work a lethal one-two even when they're bickering about who called it first.

Looks up to Bull, tries too hard to impress Doc, hasn't decided yet if Lee is a mentor or a rival.

## Group Dynamic
This isn't a family — it's a crew that's stayed together because the work has been good and nobody's died yet. Doc keeps them alive and honest. Bull keeps them disciplined. Riot keeps them moving. Lee keeps them paid and, so far, keeps them unseen when it matters. How long that holds is an open question — this is a gritty, political setting, and contracts on the frontier rarely stay clean.
