# Triggers Reference (Core Rulebook)

Triggers apply to skill checks in narrative play (never attacks, saves, or other rolls), and only your pilot's personal ability/training/background — not anything that relies on the mech (that's mech skills instead). Each roll can only benefit from one trigger at a time. Full list below.

- **Act Unseen or Unheard** — Get somewhere or do something without detection: hide, sneak, move quietly, infiltrate while avoiding patrols/cameras, sleight of hand (pickpocketing, unholstering guns, cheating at cards), wear a disguise. *(Lee has this at +2)*
- **Apply Fists to Faces** — Punch someone or fight in open, brutal unarmed combat — fist fight, martial arts duel, brawl. Never subtle or quiet.
- **Assault** — Direct, overt, loud combat: fighting through a building of hostiles, trading shots in the open, boarding-action chaos.
- **Blow Something Up** — Use explosives (improvised or otherwise), weapons, or brawn to destroy something.
- **Charm** — Convince a receptive audience or use leverage (money, power, personal benefit): smooth-talk guards, win people over, sway benefactors, talk someone down, mediate, lie convincingly, impersonate someone.
- **Get a Hold of Something** — Acquire temporary or permanent allies, assets, connections through wealth or social influence; obtain information, food, materials, soldiers.
- **Get Somewhere Quickly** — Move fast without necessarily being quiet: climb, drive, pilot, swim, take a dangerous shortcut, fall safely from height, move in zero-g, chase/flee/outrun a target.
- **Hack or Fix** — Repair a device/system, or hack it open/wreck/disable/sabotage it: electronic locks, computers, omninet servers, etc. *(Lee has this at +2)*
- **Invent or Create** — Use tools/supplies to design or build something, on the fly or over time; invent new devices, tools, or approaches.
- **Investigate** — Research a subject in detail, become well-read on an issue, solve a mystery/puzzle, locate a person or object through research and detective work.
- **Lead or Inspire** — Give an inspiring speech, motivate a group into action, administer an organization effectively, command soldiers in battle.
- **Patch** — Apply medical knowledge: medicate, bandage, suture, cauterize, neutralize poison, resuscitate; or diagnose/study disease and pathogens.
- **Pull Rank** — Coerce or convince someone who respects your authority (military or otherwise) to give information, resources, or aid — possibly against their will.
- **Read a Situation** — Look for subtext, motives, or threats: discern motivations, learn who's really in charge, sense who's about to do something rash, get a gut feeling, sense a lie.
- **Show Off** — Do something flashy, cool, or impressive, usually (not exclusively) with a weapon: trick shots, shoot a hat/weapon out of someone's hand, acrobatic flourishes. *(Lee has this at +2)*
- **Spot** — Notice hidden/hard-to-make-out details, objects, or people: spot ambushes, hidden compartments, disguises; spy on distant targets; track people/vehicles.
- **Stay Cool** — Perform a task requiring concentration/dexterity/speed/precision under pressure: pick a lock under fire, disarm an explosive, unjam a gun mid-combat.
- **Survive** — Persevere through harsh/hostile/unforgiving environments: vacuum, tundra, pirate enclaves, wilderness, desert; navigation, avoiding natural hazards.
- **Take Control** — Use force, violence, will, or direct action to seize control of an object (a gun, a keycard) or a situation (forcing people to listen/calm down/stop).
- **Take Someone Out** — Kill or disable someone quickly and quietly, up close or from a distance, ideally before they notice you. *(Lee has this at +2)*
- **Threaten** — Use force or threats to make someone comply — name the demand and the consequence. May involve blackmail or leverage. Usually burns the relationship permanently.
- **Word on the Street** — Get gossip, news, or hearsay from the streets or a specific social scene.

## Creating your own
With GM approval, you can write custom triggers — more specific than these examples (e.g. "Kill in Cold Blood" or "Insult Someone"), but they shouldn't be broader than these. They should apply to specific circumstances, not general competence.

## Reminder
You don't need a trigger to make a skill check — you can always roll a flat 1d20 vs. target 10 with no bonus.
