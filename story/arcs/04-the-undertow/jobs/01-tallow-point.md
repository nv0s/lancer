# Job 1: Tallow Point

**Arc:** [The Undertow](../arc.md)
**Status:** Complete
**Contact:** none — self-directed, off intel from the Halvorsen escort

## The Lead
No contract, no client. Working off the hab-carrier's comms logs, recovered after the Halvorsen escort ambush.

Lee cracked the logs (Hack or Fix, 1d20+2 = 21 vs. an obfuscated-but-unencrypted routing mess): "the Undertow" isn't a gang, it's a clearinghouse — a fixer called **the Actuary** who launders stolen goods and sells intel from raider cells across the Corvo Reach. The same ledger that fences cargo also shows a payment for *"H.F.C. routing — Reach corridor"* three weeks before the *Long Ledger* ambush — the Actuary sold that hit too. A recurring rendezvous location surfaced in the logs: **Tallow Point**, a derelict relay/transfer station deeper in the debris field than the raiders' hideout.

Decision: hit Tallow Point directly rather than lean on Halvorsen's people first — the plan was to bring Halvorsen back their stolen cargo *and* the Actuary's name/head in the same visit.

## Recon and the Snatch
Lee went in alone, HIDDEN, working Tallow Point's exterior at mech scale first:
- **Spot check (1d20+2 = 13):** partial read — confirmed the station was live (active power core, active relay transmissions matching the ledger's pattern) and patrolled by at least two professional security rotations, but couldn't get a clean interior layout.
- **Act Unseen or Unheard (1d20+2 = 17):** pushed in closer between patrol loops and got eyes on a reinforced private compartment — the Actuary, alone, mid-transaction on a live console, selling another target's schedule in real time.

Lee dismounted to pilot scale and took the Actuary alive and quiet:
- **Take Someone Out (1d20+2 = 19):** clean, silent takedown — zip-tied and gagged before the target even registered the threat.
- **Hack or Fix (1d20+2 = 22, nat 20):** cracked the console completely. Recovered a full client ledger (months of leaked schedules across multiple buyers, not just Halvorsen), the live in-progress transaction (a buyer and raider cell mid-payment for a schedule — not yet executed), and a real identity behind the callsign: **Wren Osei**, formerly logged with a shipping insurance underwriter before the trail goes cold.
- **Exfil (1d20+2 = 12):** got back to Ungoliant with Osei in tow, but a routine security check-in on the dead console went unanswered twice — didn't trigger an alarm, but started an unspoken clock on the station noticing.
- **Stealing the ship (Hack or Fix, 1d20+2 = 21):** rather than just running, Lee doubled back and boarded the Actuary's own docked vessel, bypassed its lockouts, and got it undocked and moving before Tallow Point's security fully mobilized. Registry check showed it flagged under three separate alias registrations.

## Racing an Ambush in Progress
The live transaction from the console named a buyer and raider cell mid-payment for an unexecuted hit. The crew burned hard to intercept:
- **Riot, Get Somewhere Quickly (1d20+2 = 6):** didn't make the intercept in time to prevent the ambush — arrived mid-boarding instead of before it.

**Target:** an independent hauler, *Kestrel's Due* (Captain Priset, crew of four, no corporate affiliation), already under boarding by a Raider Captain and three Corvo Raiders (same statline as the Halvorsen escort ambush — see [`bestiary.md`](../../../../reference/bestiary.md)).

**The fight:**
- Lee opened with a Rail Gun/Assault Rifle barrage on the Captain: **nat 20 crit, Brutal talent maxed the damage (12, exactly the Captain's HP) — one-shot kill.** Follow-up Assault Rifle shot (1d20+1 = 20 vs. Evasion 7) hit a Corvo Raider for 2 damage.
- A raider returned fire on Ungoliant: **nat 1, clean miss.**
- Bull drew aggro, planting the Drake between the raiders and *Kestrel's Due*'s hull, then finished the wounded raider (1d20+1 = 8 vs. Evasion 7, hit; 1d6+2 damage = 5, exactly enough to destroy it).
- A second raider fired on Bull: **nat 1, clean miss.**
- Riot closed out the round on the last untouched raider — Duelist's Partisan bonus plus a strong roll (1d20+2 accuracy+1 Grit = 24 vs. Evasion 7) followed by max War Pike damage (6, exactly its HP). **Destroyed.**
- The last raider, alone and having just watched a captain and two crewmates die in under a minute, was talked down over comms rather than fought: Lee's flat threat roll (1d20 = 18) broke it clean — stood down without a shot fired.

**Aftermath:** *Kestrel's Due*'s crew shaken but unharmed, cargo untouched. Captain Priset had never heard the name "the Undertow" before today and doesn't believe she was personally targeted — just unlucky, or her own schedule got sold the same way Halvorsen's was. Interrogating the surrendered raider (flat 1d20 = 8, partial) confirmed he's low-level — never met the Actuary in person, cells are strictly compartmentalized — but confirmed the operating model: **the Undertow doesn't discriminate by target. Anyone's schedule is for sale to anyone paying for it.**

## Closing Out with Halvorsen
Returned to Halvorsen's depot with the recovered hideout cargo, the stolen ship, both prisoners (Wren Osei and the surrendered raider), and the full ledger data.

Halvorsen personally took the meeting. Doc cross-referenced the ledger's payment timestamps against Halvorsen's own internal dispatch logs (Read a Situation, 1d20+2 = 15 vs. 14) and identified the leak: **Teo Marsh**, a dispatch coordinator with route-planning access and debt that cleared right when the *Long Ledger*'s schedule was sold. Halvorsen took it personally and is handling Marsh internally.

**Payout, on the spot, negotiated down in favor of hardware:**
- **6,000c** cash reward (for the recovered cargo, the stolen ship, and the Undertow/leak intel combined)
- **Halvorsen Point-Defense Battery** (Heavy Cannon, ARCING, KNOCKBACK 2, R10, 2d6 kinetic) — surplus escort-fleet hardware, given to fill Bull's empty Heavy mount
- Not Halvorsen's to claim: **3,000c** loose scrip and an **encrypted data slate**, both stripped from Wren Osei's ship before handover

Both prisoners went into Halvorsen's custody — their fate from here is Halvorsen's business, not the crew's.

## Outcome
- **Tallow Point's Actuary neutralized** — Wren Osei captured alive, the local Undertow clearinghouse's operator in Halvorsen's hands.
- **Kestrel's Due saved**, no casualties.
- **The Halvorsen leak identified and resolved** (Teo Marsh, handled internally by Halvorsen).
- **Loot:** 9,000c total, Halvorsen Point-Defense Battery (Bull's new Heavy mount), an uncracked encrypted data slate.
- **Open question:** whether Wren Osei was the whole of "the Undertow" in the Corvo Reach or just its most visible node — the full client ledger implies buyers and cells beyond what's been dealt with here. Not chased yet by choice.
