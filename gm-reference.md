# GM Quick Reference

Core rules I need at my fingertips mid-session, so I stop reconstructing them from memory.

## Turn Order (no initiative rolls)
1. Players agree who acts first (usually whoever's springing the ambush / most relevant).
2. GM picks a hostile NPC to act next.
3. The player who just acted nominates the next ally to act.
4. GM picks another hostile NPC. Repeat, strictly alternating hostile/ally.
5. Once one side is fully exhausted, the remaining side's characters go in any order.
6. **Every character acts exactly once per round — track who's gone.** A round ends when everyone (both sides) has acted once. Whichever side went last starts the next round (alternation carries over).

## Actions Per Turn
One **Standard Move** (up to Speed), plus either **two Quick Actions** or **one Full Action**. Free actions and reactions are unlimited in number but only **one reaction per turn** per character taking it (their turn or someone else's).

- **Quick Actions:** Skirmish (attack w/ 1 weapon), Boost (extra move), Grapple, Hide, Search, Scan, Lock On, Invade (tech attack), Improvised Attack, Prepare, Disengage, Activate (quick version), Mount/Dismount/Eject, Shut Down, Self-Destruct.
- **Full Actions:** Barrage (attack w/ 2 weapons or 1 Superheavy), Stabilize, Full Tech, Skill Check, Boot Up, Activate (full version), Mount/Dismount/Eject (full version).

## Pre-Combat Setup (established house precedent)
Positioning that happens *before* the first shot — being dug into cover with a weapon sighted, deploying passive systems like Turret Drones while still HIDDEN and undetected — does **not** consume a turn's actions. Combat's first turn begins with the opening attack, not the setup. Apply this consistently.

## Stabilize (Full Action)
Choose one: clear all heat + EXPOSED, **or** mark 1 Repair to restore all HP. Also the way to reload LOADING weapons.

## Key Tags
- **LOADING** — must reload after each use (via Stabilize or specific systems) before firing again.
- **ORDNANCE** — must fire before moving or taking any other action that turn; can't target engaged characters; can't be used for Overwatch.
- **ACCURATE** — +1 Accuracy (1d6) on attacks with this weapon. **INACCURATE** — same but +1 Difficulty instead.
- **AP (Armor-Piercing)** — ignores Armor.
- **ARCING** — no line of sight needed (just a traceable path), but still affected by cover.
- **SMART** — targets E-Defense instead of Evasion; can't be dodged, only jammed.
- **OVERKILL** — damage dice that roll 1 deal 1 heat to the attacker, then reroll (can chain).
- **THREAT X** — can be used for Overwatch/melee within X spaces.
- **THROWN X** — melee weapon can be thrown within X spaces; must be retrieved to reuse.

## HIDDEN
Can't be targeted by hostile attacks; enemies only know approximate location. Broken by: attacking (any type), forcing a save, the mech taking a hostile action, using Boost, or taking a reaction. **Not** broken merely by entering line of sight or losing cover mid-turn (that's what Infiltrator's Prowl protects further). Search = contested SYSTEMS (searcher) vs. AGILITY (hider).

## Accuracy / Difficulty
Each point = roll 1d6 (don't add multiples of the same type together — take the highest). Accuracy and Difficulty cancel 1:1. Max effective swing is +6/-6.

## Critical Hits
Natural 20+ on an attack roll. Roll **all** damage dice twice, keep the highest result from each individual die (e.g., 2d6 becomes 4d6, keep the best 2).

## Proximity vs. Remote Triggers
A proximity-rigged trap/mine goes off automatically when the trigger condition is met — no one spends an action on it. A **remote-detonated** device does cost the triggering character a quick action (Activate) when they press it.

## Reaction Effects Tied to Multiple Instances
Effects like Turret Drones' "Turret Attack" reaction are limited **per instance**, not globally — each deployed turret gets its own 1/round use, and multiple can trigger off the same qualifying hit if each individually has range/LOS to the target.

## Downtime Actions
One per downtime period unless it spans a long time. Named outcomes at three tiers: 9 or less / 10-19 / 20+. Full Repair needs 10+ uninterrupted hours in a secure location — resets everything (HP, Structure, Stress, Repairs, Limited uses, CP).
