# Campaign Log

**System:** LANCER Core Rulebook (Massif Press)
**Tone:** Gritty and political — full setting weight
**Format:** Sandbox. Player: Lee "Envy" Cadar. GM-run squad: Doc, Bull, Riot.

## Setting Primer
5016u. Humanity has spread across the galaxy for millennia, governed at its core by Union — a post-capital utopia trying to live up to its own ideals from a comfortable distance. Outside the Core, the galaxy runs on manna, blink gates, and the omninet, and five major manufacturers (GMS, IPS-Northstar, Smith-Shimano Corpro, HORUS, and Harrison Armory) arm the states, militias, and mercenary companies that fill the gaps Union doesn't reach. The frontier is not utopia. It's contested, exploited, and armed to the teeth.

## House Rules
- **Salvage Mercy:** a failed loot/salvage roll still yields something minor, never a total whiff. After two failed salvage attempts in a row, the third auto-succeeds at full value, no roll needed.

## Session 0 — Setup (2026-07-20)

**Starting location: Rill's End**, a Diasporan mining colony on the outer edge of charted space — tidally locked, one habitable strip, and rich enough in rare ore to be worth fighting over. Officially self-administered. Practically, it's been getting squeezed for two years by **Cassian Vantage Security**, a private contractor running on a Harrison Armory paper trail, who "license" mining claims and charge "transit tariffs" that have no basis in any law Union would recognize — and no Union administrator has shown up to say otherwise.

The crew has landed on a contract from a claims cooperative that's tired of paying. It looks like a simple escort/deterrence job. It is not going to stay simple — Cassian Vantage's muscle is better armed than a rent-a-cop outfit has any business being, and someone is bankrolling that gap.

### Status
- Contract struck with Imogen Sato (claims cooperative): reduced cash retainer + binding claim on whatever's recovered from Cassian Vantage's impound depot. Salvage has no clean title — political risk, not just cash.
- A Harrison Armory "advisor" is embedded with Vantage directly — confirmed by a captured pilot. The tariff racket goes higher than a local contractor.
- Lee's mech named: **Ungoliant**.
- Plan in motion: bleed Vantage's patrols over several days (deniable hits, no direct move on the colony) to provoke overextension, then hit the depot. Doc is coordinating with Sato to watch for retaliation against colonists.

### Hits Logged
1. **Tariff collection route, ambush 1** — 2 walkers destroyed, 1 captured and interrogated (override/shutdown codes for the walker line; confirmed a partial distress call got out; depot is guarded by at least one real mech, frame unknown). Pilot released unarmed, day's walk from home, no comms.
2. **Okonkwo claim, ambush 2** — arrived mid-shakedown, civilian claimholder held at gunpoint. 2 walkers destroyed (one a critical hit), third talked down via threat and released the hostage. Claimholder unharmed; agreed to discretion and passed a lead: an unidentified second outfit has been seen operating alongside Vantage (unrecognized insignia on a transport). Third pilot interrogated for minor intel — Vantage may be consolidating patrols back to the depot instead of continuing collections.
3. **Trap set** — captured walker from ambush 2 stripped and rigged (via the override codes) with a fake distress beacon and a remote-triggered overload, baiting whoever responds. Comm logs also yielded a transponder ID tied to the unidentified second outfit — first real lead on who they are.

4. **Trap sprung** — Vantage sent a real response: 2 Collector Walkers + a licensed Vantage Enforcer pilot, first serious opposition faced. Proximity charge caught both walkers in the opening blast. Full firefight followed — Enforcer and both walkers destroyed, no survivors/prisoners this round. Doc took a critical hit (crit from a walker) but stabilized clear of danger. Ungoliant took return fire, ended at 8/12 HP. Confirms Vantage is willing to commit real assets to protect this route now — the pressure campaign is working.

5. **Depot recon (Lee, solo, night)** — Salvage attempt on the Enforcer wreck and a trace on the mystery transponder ID both failed; the trace may have tipped off the second outfit that someone's looking into them. Recon itself: got the gate, guard tower, and motor pool layout, and confirmed patrol behavior, but tripped an old proximity sensor (evaded a search) and was later directly spotted by a guard. Killed the guard to avoid a wider alarm, got a glimpse into the hangar before pulling out — a large Harrison Armory-pattern mech, energy-armed, confirmed as the depot's real defender. Escaped clean, but the depot is now alerted that someone was there. No more free ambushes on this target.

6. **Downtime in Rill's End** — Full repair on the crew (couple of days, secure downtime). Checked in with Sato: no retaliation against the colony so far. She flagged the depot's resupply convoy (due in ~2 days) as a soft target — cutting it could starve the depot's defenses, possibly including the second outfit's own resupply. Lee dug through local shipping/customs records and identified the second outfit: **"Ossuary Freight"** — a shell company, thin cover, clean-money financing, active alongside Vantage only since the tariff racket escalated. The digging wasn't invisible; Ossuary will eventually know their records were accessed.

7. **Resupply convoy ambush** — Clean sweep. Lee opened with a turret drone (pre-combat setup) and two rifle shots, dropping the Ossuary Handler to critical before the turret finished it off outright. Both Vantage escort walkers destroyed shortly after (Riot, Bull, Doc each landed a kill). No losses, minimal damage taken. Cracked the two undefended cargo transports: found HA-pattern reactor cells/energy ammunition matching the hangar mech, plus **shipping manifests with Harrison Armory logistics codes laundered through Ossuary Freight** — hard documentary proof HA is directly arming Vantage's operation, not just observing. Also picked up a functional kinetic sidearm and a cache of sellable HA-pattern hardware.

8. **Vantage depot assault** — Lee opened with all 3 Turret Drones pre-deployed, then two shots on the HA Siege Frame (Size 3, HP 22): first shot + all three turrets triggering off it (20 damage in one exchange), second shot finished it before it ever fired a single shot. Remaining 3 Collector Walkers mopped up over 2 rounds, no crew losses. **Depot fully cleared.**

**Loot:** Sato's contracted claim fulfilled — two years of impounded mining equipment, vehicles, and materiel recovered for the co-op. From the Siege Frame wreckage: reactor/weapon system components, and an intact **flight recorder with HA unit designation and deployment authorization data** — hard proof Harrison Armory knowingly deployed military-grade hardware to enforce Vantage's illegal tariff racket. Lee personally picked up a functioning HA energy sidearm (trophy-grade, needs HA license to use properly).
